Call for Papers

AI Discovery in the Wild invites papers, posters, and demos on methods, systems, evaluation, and deployment for AI discovery beyond benchmark settings.

ACM Overleaf Template Back to Main Page
01

Submission tracks

Paper Track

  • Short papers: 2-4 pages, excluding references.
  • Full papers: 6-9 pages, excluding references.
  • Suitable for new methods, evaluation studies, infrastructure, and practice-driven reports.

Poster Track

  • Poster proposals: 1-2 pages, excluding references.
  • Ideal for emerging directions, exploratory results, and systems ideas not yet in full-paper form.
  • Accepted paper submissions may also be presented as posters.

Demo Track

  • Demo proposals: 1-2 pages, excluding references.
  • Submissions should describe the system, intended live setup, and what attendees will learn.
  • We especially welcome end-to-end discovery workflows and evaluation tooling.
02

Format and template

Format

  • Authors should use the ACM acmart SIGCONF template.
  • Submissions should be uploaded as PDF files in a two-column conference layout.
  • The paper should include a concise abstract, main text, and references.
  • Appendices are allowed, but reviewers are not required to read them.
  • Figures, tables, and system diagrams should be legible in a conference-style layout.

Template

Official ACM SIGCONF Overleaf template
  • Use the official ACM SIGCONF Overleaf template as the starting point.
  • For submission, authors should prepare an anonymous review version.

03

Policies

Double-Blind Review

  • The paper track is double blind. Author names and affiliations should be omitted from the review copy.
  • Where possible, code repositories, websites, and artifacts should be anonymized for review.
  • Poster and demo submissions should also avoid unnecessary self-identifying text in the review version.

Non-Archival

  • This workshop is intended to be non-archival and does not plan formal proceedings.
  • Submissions under review elsewhere, recently submitted conference papers, and previously posted preprints are welcome.
  • If the work is under review or previously posted, authors should disclose that status at submission time.

Dates and review

Key Dates

  • Notification: May 5, 2026
  • Final materials: May 15, 2026

  • Submission portal: TBD

Decisions and Presentation

  • Each submission will receive at least two reviews from the program committee.
  • Reviews will evaluate relevance, technical soundness, clarity, novelty, and discussion value.
  • Accepted papers will be included in the workshop program and may be selected for spotlight talks.
  • Accepted paper submissions can also be accompanied by posters; accepted poster and demo proposals will appear in the corresponding session.
  • A Best Paper Award will recognize one especially strong submission.
